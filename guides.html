Game Rules:

  1. The rules, regulations and by-laws of Mississauga Chess Club comply with the guidelines of FIDE and our Club Committee
  2. As a member you must accept these conditions and respect MCC referees and club officials, who run the Club on a volunteer basis
  3. Respect all participants. Don’t engage in psychological tricks such as staring at the opponent while he is thinking, coughing across the table at them, or intentionally failing to properly adjust your pieces
  4. You must abstain from verbal abuse or deliberately provoking an opponent or other player nearby
  5. Always introduce yourself to your opponents, shake hands before and after the game, and accept a loss gracefully
  6. Smoking or consumption of alcohol is strictly forbidden. It is prohibited to enter the Club while under the influence of alcohol
  7. Failure to respect the facilities, equipment and venues provided by MCC and other organizers will be considered a serious breach of the Page 2 of 3 code of conduct. This includes a responsibility to clean up after themselves in matters relating to house-keeping, hygiene and cleanliness
  8. Prior to play, everyone should please sign in at the reception area, pay any event fee, and proceed to the playing area
  9. At the end of play, please leave the playing area as you found it
  10. Chess pieces, boards and clocks furnished by the Club should be treated with care
  11. MCC members shall practice good sportsmanship and fair play and shall be courteous to other players. MCC will not tolerate any kind of bullying behavior
  12. Parents are responsible for their children, well being and safety. The MCC cannot be held liabile for any injury, death, or other events that take place
  13. During tournaments and club activities, members and visitors are expected to adhere to all rules and regulations as stated in this document
  14. Cell phones must be turned off when entering the playing areas. Electronic devices, including but not limited to, media players, handheld video game devices, etc., are strictly prohibited
  15. Violation of Club rules will result in a verbal warning. Continued violation of rules will result in a written reprimand to be sent home. Subsequent violation of these rules may result in expulsion from the Club
  16. Parents with concerns regarding any Club activity, method of instruction, fairness of play, coaches, child conduct or behavior etc., should contact the Club officers directly for discussion before or after Chess club meetings
  17. Members must leave the chess room the way they found it
  18. MCC tournaments rules is governed by FIDE’s Official Rules of Chess. Any disputes regarding match play must be brought to the attention of a Club arbiter or Club TD who will then make a ruling based on the official rules. The Club’s ruling is final
  19. Rulebooks are available upon request
